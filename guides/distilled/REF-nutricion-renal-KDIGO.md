## KDIGO 2024 — Sección 3.3: Diet

**Practice Point 3.3.1** "Advise people with CKD to adopt healthy and diverse diets with a
higher consumption of plant-based foods compared to animal-based foods and a lower
consumption of ultraprocessed foods."
[ES] Aconsejar dietas sanas y diversas, con mayor consumo de alimentos de origen vegetal
que animal y menor consumo de ultraprocesados.

**Practice Point 3.3.2** "Use renal dietitians or accredited nutrition providers to educate
people with CKD about dietary adaptations regarding sodium, phosphorus, potassium, and
protein intake, tailored to their individual needs, and severity of CKD and other comorbid
conditions."
[ES] Educar sobre adaptaciones de sodio, fósforo, potasio y proteína según necesidades
individuales y severidad.

### 3.3.1 Protein intake

**Recommendation 3.3.1.1** "We suggest maintaining a protein intake of 0.8 g/kg body
weight/d in adults with CKD G3–G5 (2C)."
[ES] Se sugiere mantener 0.8 g/kg/día de proteína en adultos con ERC G3–G5.

**Practice Point 3.3.1.1** "Avoid high protein intake (>1.3 g/kg body weight/d) in adults
with CKD at risk of progression."
[ES] Evitar ingesta alta de proteína (>1.3 g/kg/día) en riesgo de progresión.

**Practice Point 3.3.1.2** "In adults with CKD who are willing and able, and who are at risk
of kidney failure, consider prescribing, under close supervision, a very low–protein diet
(0.3–0.4 g/kg body weight/d) supplemented with essential amino acids or ketoacid analogs
(up to 0.6 g/kg body weight/d)."
[ES] En riesgo de falla renal, considerar bajo supervisión dieta muy baja en proteína
0.3–0.4 g/kg/día con aminoácidos esenciales o cetoanálogos (hasta 0.6 g/kg/día).

**Practice Point 3.3.1.3** "Do not prescribe low- or very low–protein diets in metabolically
unstable people with CKD."
[ES] No prescribir dietas bajas o muy bajas en proteína en pacientes metabólicamente inestables.

**Practice Point 3.3.1.4** "Do not restrict protein intake in children with CKD due to the
risk of growth impairment..."
[ES] No restringir proteína en niños con ERC por riesgo de retraso del crecimiento.

**Practice Point 3.3.1.5** "In older adults with underlying conditions such as frailty and
sarcopenia, consider higher protein and calorie dietary targets."
[ES] En adultos mayores con fragilidad o sarcopenia, considerar objetivos más altos de
proteína y calorías.

### 3.3.2 Sodium intake

**Recommendation 3.3.2.1** "We suggest that sodium intake be <2 g of sodium per day (or <90
mmol of sodium per day, or <5 g of sodium chloride per day) in people with CKD (2C)."
[ES] Se sugiere sodio < 2 g/día (< 90 mmol/día, o < 5 g/día de cloruro de sodio).

**Practice Point 3.3.2.1** "Dietary sodium restriction is usually not appropriate for
patients with sodium-wasting nephropathy."
[ES] La restricción de sodio no suele ser apropiada en nefropatía perdedora de sal.

## KDIGO 2024 — otros puntos dietéticos

**Practice Point 3.11.5.2** "Provide advice to limit the intake of foods rich in bioavailable
potassium (e.g., processed foods) for people with CKD G3–G5 who have a history of
hyperkalemia or as a prevention strategy during disease periods in which hyperkalemia risk
may be a concern."
[ES] Limitar alimentos ricos en potasio biodisponible (p. ej., ultraprocesados) en ERC G3–G5
con antecedente de hiperkalemia.

**Practice Point 3.15.1.5** "Consider a plant-based 'Mediterranean-style' diet in addition to
lipid-modifying therapy to reduce cardiovascular risk."
[ES] Considerar dieta mediterránea basada en vegetales, además del tratamiento hipolipemiante.

**Practice Point 3.10.1** "In people with CKD, consider use of pharmacological treatment with
or without dietary intervention to prevent development of acidosis with potential clinical
implications (e.g., serum bicarbonate <18 mmol/l in adults)."
[ES] Considerar tratamiento (con o sin intervención dietética) para prevenir acidosis
(p. ej., bicarbonato < 18 mmol/L).

## KDIGO 2026 (BORRADOR) — Sección 3.9: Nutritional intervention in AKI

**Practice Point 3.9.1** "Nutrition should be provided preferentially via the enteral route
in children and adults at risk of or with AKI or AKD."
[ES] Proveer nutrición preferentemente por vía enteral en riesgo de o con LRA/AKD.

**Practice Point 3.9.2** "Avoid restriction of protein intake with the aim of preventing or
delaying initiation of RRT in children and adults with AKI or AKD."
[ES] Evitar restringir proteína para prevenir/retrasar el inicio de terapia de reemplazo renal.

**Practice Point 3.9.3** "Excessive protein intake should be avoided in adults with AKI or AKD."
[ES] Evitar ingesta excesiva de proteína en adultos con LRA/AKD.

**Practice Point 3.9.4** "Caloric targets do not need to be changed due to AKI or AKD in
children or adults, unless they are receiving RRT."
[ES] Las metas calóricas no cambian por LRA/AKD salvo que reciban terapia de reemplazo renal.

**Practice Point 3.9.5** "Regular review and supplementation of water-soluble vitamins and
micronutrients should be considered in children and adults receiving continuous RRT (CRRT)."
[ES] Revisar y suplementar vitaminas hidrosolubles y micronutrientes en CRRT.
